History of Feijoada

Feijoada is a traditional Brazilian dish with roots in Portuguese cuisine. It originated as a hearty meal for slaves in Brazil during the colonial era.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of black beans
  • 1 pound of pork ribs
  • 1 pound of smoked sausage
  • 1 pound of bacon
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the black beans and soak them overnight in water.
  2. In a large pot, cook the pork ribs in water until tender. Remove the ribs and set them aside.
  3. In the same pot, cook the smoked sausage and bacon until browned. Remove them and set them aside.
  4. In the same pot, saut√© the chopped onion and minced garlic until fragrant.
  5. Add the soaked black beans, bay leaves, and enough water to cover the beans. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 2 hours or until the beans are tender.
  6. Add the cooked pork ribs, smoked sausage, and bacon back to the pot. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Simmer for another 30 minutes.
  7. Serve the Feijoada hot with white rice, collard greens, and orange slices.
